Pizza

Description

There are two main steps to making a pizza. The first is making the crust. Which usually take longer than the other step. The second step is the toppings.
Usually tomato sauce and cheese (plus any others you want to add to the top).

Directions

Once your pizzas are loaded with toppings, it's time to fire up the grill! Unless you have an outdoor pizza oven (see below), an outdoor grill is your
best bet for backyard pizza. Using a pizza stone or steel is going to help you mimic that wood-fired taste. Baking pizza on aluminum foil will also
work, but it won't produce quite the same crust.

  1. Insert pizza stone and preheat grill. Place your pizza stone or steel (or a sheet of aluminum foil) directly on the grill grates. Close cover and preheat the grill on high heat.
  2. Slide pizza into stone. Once the grill is heated, use a pizza peel that is dusted with flour or cornmeal to slide your assembled pizza onto your stone or foil. Close the grill cover.
  3. Grill the pizza until the crust is browned, about 10 to 12 minutes. Use either a pair of tongs or a pizza peel to remove the pizza from the grill, and slice.
